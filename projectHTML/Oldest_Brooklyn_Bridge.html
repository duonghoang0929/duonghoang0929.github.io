Brooklyn Bridge

Information

Content Information

Brooklyn Bridge is one of the oldest suspension bridges in the US, about 1,825m long across the East River connecting the cities of Manhattan and Brooklyn. Once completed, this is the largest suspension bridge in the world and the first steel suspension bridge. This project was designed by architect John Augustus Roebling and was completed by his son Washington Augustus Roebling after 14 years (1869 - 1883). Since opening, the bridge has become an iconic part of New York City. In 1964, the bridge was designated a national historical heritage.
