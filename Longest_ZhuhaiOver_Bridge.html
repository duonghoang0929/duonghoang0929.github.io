Content Information

Bridge Hong Kong - Zhuhai - Macao (HZMB: Hong Kong-Zhuhai-Macau Bridge) is a system of bridges , tunnels 55 km long including a series of three cable-stayed bridge, viaduct, tunnel under the sea and four islands artificial island. It is the longest sea route in the world, across the Lingding and Jiuzhou canals and connecting Hong Kong with Macau and Zhuhai , the three major cities to the west of the Pearl River Delta . HZMB was designed to last 120 years and was built at a cost of ¥ 126.9 billion ( Unknown country code: China $ billion ). The cost of construction of the main bridge is estimated at ¥ 51.1 billion ( Unknown country code: China $ billion ) financed by bank loans and shared among mainland Chinese governments, Hong Kong and Macau. The bridge was originally planned to open for traffic in October 2016, however by February 6, 2018 the structure was completed and journalists were on tour. mandarin. On October 24, 2018, Chinese President Xi Jinping attended the opening ceremony of the Hong Kong-Zhuhai-Macau Bridge at Zhuhai Port..

The structure consists of a 5.5-kilometer (3.4 mi) long undersea tunnel (the world's longest) with artificial islands. According to the Arup project design team, this is China's first bridge project to coordinate with the tunnel. However, the design firm says the bridge is only 35 kilometers in total; The reason for the difference in numbers is not known. The work started with accretion to create a 540-acre artificial island off the coast of Zhuhai. This is the point where the customs authorities will control the movement between the three places. Most structures are prefabricated, for example, the girder will be made available at the same time the bridge is being built. The tunnel is also built from 100 meters of prefabricated sections. Zhu Yongling, an official in charge of the construction project, said, "The bridge is designed to be about 120 years old, can withstand winds of up to 51 meters per second, or the equivalent of rung 16 (184). to 201 km / h), the maximum step of the Beaufort wind measuring system .
